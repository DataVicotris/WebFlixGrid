Jim Carrey

Jim Carrey, born James Eugene Carrey on January 17, 1962, in Newmarket, Ontario, Canada, is a Canadian-American actor and comedian known for his energetic slapstick performances. He gained recognition in the early 1990s with his roles in the sketch comedy television series "In Living Color" and went on to star in a series of successful films such as "Ace Ventura: Pet Detective," "The Mask," and "Dumb and Dumber".

Carrey is also known for his dramatic roles, including his critically acclaimed performances in "The Truman Show" and "Man on the Moon," for which he won Golden Globe Awards. He has continued to act in both comedic and dramatic roles, with notable films like "Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind" and "Bruce Almighty"
